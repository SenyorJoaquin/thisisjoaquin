Independent Journalism

Rebel Atlas Media

Role: Lead Developer Stack: WordPress, Custom CSS

A high-stakes editorial platform built for independent journalists to share deep-dive stories with a global audience.

The Architecture

Focused on a 'Brutalist Newspaper' aesthetic. I utilized heavy typography and a modular grid to ensure that long-form content remains readable while maintaining a premium, authoritative feel. The backend was customized to handle high-resolution imagery and complex editorial layouts without sacrificing load speed.

The Trenches

The primary challenge was balancing the 'Vice Media' inspired visual weight with the need for accessibility. Every custom CSS module was stress-tested for responsiveness to ensure the editorial impact remains consistent from desktop to mobile.

The Study

Rebel Atlas Media served as one of the most politically sensitive and emotionally charged projects I've had the privilege to work on. The main question here was, "How can I present these stories in a way that still encapsulates the gravity of the subject matter while remaining accessible to a broad audience, all while keeping the visual identity of the brand?"

With this in mind, I focused on creating a visual language that was both bold and accessible, highlighting the raw beauty of their accompanying photographs. Taking inspiration from Vice Media's no non-sense approach to visual storytelling, I crafted a User Interface (UI) that functions as a curated digital map. This interface was designed to make users feel as if they are viewing an insider magazine, or moving from outsiders into the intimate, hidden stories found inside Rebel Atlas.

Now moving back to the emotional aspect. It has been proven that emotion is a primary driving force influencing behavior. Therefore, to convert a user into a subscriber, the platform itself must invoke feelings of exclusivity, righteousness, and being a "rebel." This is, after all, what they are supporting. This is when strategic copywriting techniques come into play, and that is what I made sure to implement throughout the structure.

To ground this emotional and editorial vision, I moved beyond standard templates into custom implementation. This approach allowed for the required "raw" aesthetic while ensuring technical robustness for long-form content. I established a precise visual hierarchy based on custom typographic rules. Utilizing the site's code-level styling capabilities, I imported Oswald Display for bold, assertive headlines and capitalized navigation, paired with Roboto for clean, readable body text across all articles.

My UX decisions focused on minimizing friction while maintaining narrative momentum. I implemented a continuous "independent // global // long-form" repeating ticker banner that frames the entire experience, creating a recognizable aesthetic signature that appears consistently across the homepag and landing pages. For deep-dive immersion, I custom-coded a transition-driven hero section, where images of conflict, like an AK-47 muzzle carrying a flower, hold maximum visual weight. To solve for text legibility without compromising photography, I utilized precise linear-gradient overlays directly on the images to transparent).

The final component was the implementation of specific copywriting and strategic UI interactions to trigger the subscriber prompt. We executed "Conversion by Righteousness." The visual experience establishes an alternative reality to "mainstream outlets," and the copywriting translates that feeling into action. The conversion interaction points utilize high-impact contrast and language. For example, on the main support page, I implemented a definitive statement: "CORPORATE MEDIA IS COMPROMISED. WE AREN'T." This is immediately followed by copy emphasizing the real-world gravity of the journalists (mentioning those "risking their safety"). We then map that emotional gravity directly to the final action step: the yellow "Donate to us" button, turning the user's emotional reaction (righteousness, connection to the story) into the conversion moment.
