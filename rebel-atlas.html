Independent Journalism

Rebel Atlas Media

Role: Lead Developer Stack: WordPress, Custom CSS Visit Live Site

A high-stakes editorial platform built for independent journalists to share deep-dive stories with a global audience.

The Architecture

Focused on a 'Brutalist Newspaper' aesthetic. I utilized heavy typography and a modular grid to ensure that long-form content remains readable while maintaining a premium, authoritative feel. The backend was customized to handle high-resolution imagery and complex editorial layouts without sacrificing load speed.

The Trenches

The primary challenge was designing for a brand that strictly calls for a bold, rebellious, and entirely non-traditional digital experience. Standard web conventions had to be broken to ensure that striking visual images and the hard-hitting articles remained the absolute priority across the entire layout. Every user flow and interface choice had to capture this raw visual weight while seamlessly structuring the site to entice and compel readers to buy the physical magazine.

The Study

The Core Objective

Rebel Atlas Media served as one of the most politically sensitive and emotionally charged projects I've had the privilege to work on. The main question here was, "How can I present these stories in a way that still encapsulates the gravity of the subject matter while remaining accessible to a broad audience, all while keeping the visual identity of the brand?" The definitive answer lay in treating powerful visual imagery and raw, bold statements as the single most critical components of the entire platform.

The UX & Visual Identity

With this in mind, the interface design deliberately steps away from text-dense layouts to elevate striking, uncompromised visual media into the primary narrative driver. Taking inspiration from Vice Media's no-nonsense approach to visual storytelling, I crafted a User Interface (UI) that functions as a curated digital map. This interface was designed to make users feel as if they are viewing an insider magazine, relying on imagery to bridge the gap between outside observers and the intimate, hidden realities found inside Rebel Atlas.

Beyond the visuals, bold and provocative written statements serve as the backbone of the platform's user engagement strategy. Emotion is the primary catalyst for user behavior; therefore, to convert a casual reader into a dedicated subscriber, the typography must instantly command authority. Unapologetic, high-contrast statements are strategically weaponized across the layout to invoke feelings of exclusivity, righteousness, and active resistance—reminding the audience exactly what kind of independent truth they are supporting.

The Technical Execution

To ground this vision, I moved beyond standard templates into a custom implementation that perfectly balances this aggressive editorial style with technical robustness. I established a precise visual hierarchy dictated by custom typographic rules, utilizing code-level styling capabilities to import Oswald Display for the assertive, capitalized headlines and navigation, paired with Roboto for clean, readable body text across long-form entries.

Every interactive UX decision was tailored to minimize friction while maximizing the narrative momentum of the visuals. I implemented a continuous "independent // global // long-form" repeating ticker banner that frames the entire experience, creating a recognizable aesthetic signature. For deep-dive immersion, I custom-coded a transition-driven hero section where high-impact imagery—such as an AK-47 muzzle carrying a flower—holds maximum visual weight, using precise linear-gradient overlays on the images to transition cleanly to transparent background layers for flawless text legibility.

The Conversion Framework

The final component was executing "Conversion by Righteousness" by pairing these massive visuals directly with confrontational copywriting. The user interface establishes a stark alternative to mainstream outlets, leveraging definitive, bold statements to demand action. On the support page, a massive typography block declares: "CORPORATE MEDIA IS COMPROMISED. WE AREN'T." This statement shifts focus directly to the real-world gravity of journalists risking their safety on the ground, mapping that emotional intensity straight to a high-visibility yellow "Donate to us" action button to secure the conversion.
