Saling-Kamay

Tech: KP-LSTM / Machine Learning Stack: Python, MediaPipe, TensorFlow GitHub Repository

A foundational research project and assistive system designed to bridge the communication gap between the Deaf and hearing communities in the Philippines through Filipino Sign Language (FSL) translation.

The Context

The lack of research advancements in assistive technologies centered around FSL has created a massive awareness gap. Most existing systems overlook the unique grammatical structure and cultural nuances of the language, utilizing narrow datasets that fail to reflect the dynamic vocabulary of the Filipino Deaf community.

The Architecture

Saling-Kamay is built upon a custom-developed video dataset of over 100 conversational FSL signs. The system utilizes two primary technical prototypes: (1) a Hand Keypoints and Long Short-Term Memory (KP-LSTM) gesture recognition model to capture the fluid movement of signs, and (2) a specialized Natural Language Processing (NLP) algorithm designed for FSL-to-Filipino grammar translation.

The Trenches

Developing a foundational resource meant starting from scratch. Mapping FSL’s distinct grammar into a predictive model required extensive dataset curation and technical stress-testing. By focusing on hand keypoint tracking rather than just raw video frames, the model achieves higher accuracy in recognizing the structural nuances that define FSL as a complete and independent language.
