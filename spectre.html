The Architecture

Specter operates on the principle of frequency-domain manipulation, treating a digital image as a physical coordinate system. The vertical (Y) axis maps to frequency distribution, while the horizontal (X) axis represents time. The luminance of any given pixel dictates the amplitude (volume) of its corresponding frequency.

The synthesis utilizes a modified Inverse Fast Fourier Transform (IFFT) logic. Instead of performing a standard transformation, the engine employs a logarithmic oscillator bank. This aligns the output frequencies (ranging from 200Hz to 20kHz) to the logarithmic nature of human hearing perception. Each pixel essentially triggers a sine wave for a precise duration:

s(t) = Σ An sin(2π fn t) * brightness

This is computationally expensive, as thousands of oscillators must be calculated and summed in real-time. By utilizing logarithmic scaling, we ensure that higher frequencies are not disproportionately represented in the acoustic carrier.

System SOP / Tutorial

  1. Prepare Source Image

    Prepare a sharp, high-contrast monochrome image. High frequency definition is achieved by using areas of pure white against deep black. The system resolves images at 400x200 pixel resolution.

  2. Load Source

    Utilize the 'Source Selection' interface to load your prepared image. The engine will render a pre-scan onto the signal stage for operational verification.

  3. Initiate Synthesis

    Execute 'Encode to Freq'. The system will iterate through all 80,000 pixels in the image matrix, assigning sine wave parameters. The forensic analysis reveals that this step requires significant browser processing resources.

  4. Forensic Analysis

    Upon successful playback, the resulting audio is a 5-second "textural" noise burst. To verify the hidden visual payload, analyze this audio stream using a professional FFT spectrogram utility (e.g., Audacity or specialized signal intelligence software).
